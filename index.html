The U.S. Prior Authorization Problem

Prior authorization is necessary but burdensome. Regulators push ePA and transparency by 2027, while clinical and financial risks continue to mount.

High-Cost Procedures

Primary TKA ≈ $24.5k
Revision TKA ≈ $43k
Primary THA ≈ $26k
Revision THA ≈ $53k

Revision surgeries cost nearly double, making accurate initial decisions critical for cost management.

Source: U.S. cost benchmarks, PubMed studies

4.8% 30-day Readmissions
~1% PJI within 12 months

After TKA, serious adverse events and readmissions are material risks that impact both patient outcomes and costs.

MCID (Minimal Clinically Important Difference)

The smallest change in a treatment outcome that a patient would identify as important. For WOMAC scores, ΔWOMAC ≥ 10 points represents meaningful clinical improvement.

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)

A method to explain individual predictions by computing the contribution of each feature to the prediction. Provides transparent, interpretable AI explanations.

PAS (Prior Authorization Support)

HL7 FHIR implementation guide for electronic prior authorization workflows, enabling seamless integration between providers and payers.
